This is Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded vertebrates that belong to the class Reptilia. They are known for their scaly skin, and many species lay eggs. Reptiles are ectothermic, meaning they rely on external heat sources to regulate their body temperature. They can be found in various habitats, including deserts, forests, wetlands, and even oceans.Reptiles play important ecological roles as predators and prey, helping maintain the balance of ecosystems. Many species have developed adaptations for survival, including camouflage, venom, or protective shells.Most reptiles are ectothermic, meaning they rely on external heat sources to regulate their body temperature.

Scaly skin – which helps prevent water loss and protects them in various environments.

Cold-blooded metabolism – they absorb heat from their surroundings to regulate their body temperature.

Laying of leathery or hard-shelled eggs – though some reptiles give birth to live young.

Lungs for respiration – all reptiles breathe air through lungs.
