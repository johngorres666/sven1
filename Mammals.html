This is Mammals

Mammals are a class of animals within the Animalia kingdom that are distinguished by several unique characteristics. They are warm-blooded vertebrates, meaning they have a backbone and can regulate their body temperature independently of the environment. Mammals are highly diverse and can be found in almost every habitat on Earth, from oceans to deserts.

Mammary glands: Female mammals have specialized glands that produce milk to nourish their young.

Hair or fur: All mammals have some form of hair or fur, even if it's just during certain life stages. This helps with insulation and protection.

Warm-blooded (endothermic): Mammals regulate their internal body temperature regardless of external conditions.

Live birth: Most mammals give birth to live young (except for monotremes like the platypus and echidna, which lay eggs). Four-chambered heart: Mammals have a highly efficient circulatory system with a heart divided into four chambers.

Complex brains: Mammals typically have larger and more complex brains relative to their body size, which allows for advanced behaviors, problem-solving, and social interactions.
